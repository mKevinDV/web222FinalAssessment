About Me

My name is Kevin, and I am currently a student in the second term of the Computer Programming & Analysis program at Seneca College. I previously studied Chemistry at York University and currently work as a Night Auditor at Fairmont Royal York. Working with spreadsheets in this role is what sparked my interest in programming because I find that Excel works much like its own programming language.

Why Egyptian Ratscrew

My competitive nature drew me into this game. I like games that require fast thinking, which explains why I love playing volleyball as a libero and playing FPS video games that demand fast reaction times. I grew up playing with a childhood friend, and most of the time, it would just be us 2. That meant there was only 1 winner and 1 loser. Neither of us would win more than 3 games in a row, going back and forth in wins and losses. We would always rub the loss in the other person's face but losing always fueled me to try harder the next game.

I've learned to translate that fuel I get from losing to other aspects of my life - even when I fail, I can push myself to be even better and to overcome any obstacle.

This game is extremely simple to set up yet requires a lot of my attention. Without a doubt, it is in my top 5 favourite non-electronic games to play.
