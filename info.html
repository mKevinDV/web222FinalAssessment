History Of The Game

Although the origin of the game is unknown, there are different proposed theories surrounding the game's roots. The prevailing theory is that is that it is a combination of Beggar-My-Neighbour, a game known as early as the 1900s, and Slapjack, and infamous card-slapping game. Egyptian Ratscrew gained popularity in the 1990s.

How To Play

  • Number Of Players: 2+
  • What You Need: Deck of 52 cards
  • Objective Of The Game: To be the last player with all 52 cards
  • To Start: Cards are evenly dealed, one at a time, face down to each player. Each player will hold their deck of cards dealed with them face down in their hand.
Starting to the left of the dealer, each player will take turns making a play by removing the top card of the top of their deck and place it face up in the middle to create a new discard pile. When taking the top card from their own deck, they must flip the card away from them so that the card is revealed to everyone at the same time. Play proceeds to the next player with them playing their card on the top of the discard pile in a circle (clockwise) until a face card (Jack, Queen, King or Ace) is played.

    If a player plays a jack, queen, king or ace, the next player in the rotation must do the following:

  • If an ace is played, the next player has 4 chances to play a face card of their own
  • If a king is played, the next player has 3 chances to play a face card of their own
  • If a queen is played, the next player has 2 chances to play a face card of their own
  • If a jack is played, the next player has 1 chance to play a face card of their own
If the next player does not play a face card within their allotted chances, then the last player who played a face card will receive the entire discard pile.

For example, if Player 1 plays a queen, Player 2 has 3 chances to play a face card. If Player 2 plays a jack on their second chance, then Player 3 has 1 chance to play a face card. If Player 3 plays a 3, then Player 2 takes the entire discard pile. Player 2 will place the entire discard pile face-down and place it under their deck of cards in their hand. Player 2 will then play the top card from their deck of cards and place the card face up in the middle. The next player will then play their card and play will continue clockwise.

Once a player obtains all 52 cards, the game is over and they are declared the winner.

Now this is what makes Egyptian Ratscrew even more fun: in addition to this basic turn-based system, there are other ways to retrieve the discard pile and build up the amount of cards in your hand. These are called slap rules. If any conditions for a slap rule are present, any player can slap the middle discard pile. The first player to slap the deck of cards in the middle retrieves the discard pile and places it on the bottom of their own deck and play will continue.

    There are many variations of the game that people play with, so the slap rules are created and agreed upon by all players prior to starting the game. There are the most standard slap rules that many players around the world play with:

  • Double: when two of the same cards are laid down consecutively (e.g., 10, 10)
  • Sandwich: when two cards of the same cards are played with 1 card in between them (e.g., 10, 3, 10)
  • Top Bottom: when the first card played (at the bottom of the discard pile) is the same as the last card played (at the top of the discard pile) (e.g., 10, 3, 4, 7, 10)
  • Tens: when two cards played consecutively add up to 10. An Ace would count as 1, but the other face cards (Jack, Queen, King) do not have value (e.g., 10, 2 OR A, 9)
  • Four In A Row: when four cards are played that are in ascending or descending order (e.g., 4, 5, 6, 7 OR 10, 9 8, 7)
  • Marriage: when a Queen is played before or after a King (e.g., Q, K OR K, Q)
If a pile is slapped but no conditions for a slap rule are present, then the person(s) who incorrectly slapped must add two cards from the bottom of their pile to the bottom of the discard pile. If you run out of cards, you can “slap your way back in” if any of the slap rule conditions are met. BUT if you slap when a slap rule condition is not met, then you can not re-enter the current game.

Tips & Tricks

  • Lightning Quick Reflexes: Even If you can't get lucky and play a face card every turn, then being super attentive to the slap rule conditions and slapping first will quickly build up the deck in your hand. They happen a lot more frequently than you think, so always be ready to slap!
  • Elite Memorization: Remembering the first card played in the discard pile will help prepare you to slap more quickly once the same card is played. It is also good to try and keep track of which players have which face cards and being more attentive to their play to keep them from winning. The more face cards a player has, the more likely they are to win.
  • Fierce Competitiveness: Without a drive to win, you might as well play Go Fish! You must always be ready to slap the discard pile! You can't just win on luck alone. You need to want it more than the other competitors! Nothing makes the game more exciting than every player having a competitive spirit.
